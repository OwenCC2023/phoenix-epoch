Control, Regions, and Rebellion System

Main Goal: Allow for levels of central control and complete lack thereof to be modeled within the game.

General Principles:
	- Provinces that join the nation after the start of the game may spawn rebellions if their provincial ideologies are widely opposed to the national ideologies.
	- Provinces that are unhappy and have low stability may rebel to try to leave the nation, join a neighboring nation, or form their own NPC nation.
	- Special rebellion type called Partisans can spawn in enemy-occupied provinces (stub for Occupation System).
	
	- Control can be reduced to increase stability, and decrease likelihood of Rebellion.
	- Control starts at its maximum value of 100%, and can be dropped to 1%.
	- Lowering control decreases tax revenues.

	- Regions are groups of provinces within nations.
	- Regions can have their control set at a regional level.
	

Components:
	- Rebels are a specific unit type within the military system (stub) that cost no maintenance, have no material requirements, and cannot be recruited.
	- Control applies a positive multiplier to goods produced (up to +25%) in proportion to the amount that Control is below 100%.
	- Percentage Control directly controls what percentage of taxes levied are paid to the national government. Provincial governments keep the remainder for themselves.
	- Control has no impact on policy costs.
	- Regions exist, and can have provinces removed and added to them at will by the nation.

Tie-ins with Other Systems:
	- Buildings:
		- Trade Capacity, Bureaucratic Capacity, and research generated by buildings is effected by control the same way as taxes.
	- Policies:
		- Control effects manpower from the province the same as taxes.
	- Ideologies:
		- Libertarians get a lot more stability and also happiness for being in regions with less Control.
		- Authoritarians don't like being in a Nation with provinces at less than 100% Control.
		- Egalitarians like it when all provinces are around the same level of Control.
		- 
	- Provincial Integration System:
		- Normalization is slowed by less control. Normalization time doubles for every 40% below 100% Control.
	- Espionage:
		- Lower control means lower Defense and higher Transparency.

Additional Effects:
	- Requires the tracking of province-level and region-level tax revenue, trade capacity, bureaucratic capacity, and research. These pools do not interact with anything, for now.
	- Provinces within regions have these pools tracked at the region level only. No double-counting resources.
	- Provinces outside regions have their pools tracked at the provincial level.
