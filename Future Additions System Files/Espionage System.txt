Espionage System:

Main Goal: Create a fun system that allows players to take actions to see information about foreign nations and take actions to protect their own information. 

Components:
	- Attack: Ability of a nation to view information foreign nations wish to be kept hidden. Ability to operate secretly within a foreign nation. Ability of external Actions on foreign soil.
	- Defense: Ability of a nation to prevent others from gaining information. Ability to prevent foreign operatives from accomplishing their goals. Ability of internal Actions on own soil.
	- Transparency: How much a foreign actor knows about the target nation and/or province.
	- Actions: Specific orders that can be given within the Espionage system to accomplish a specific task. Will require specific levels of Attack or Defense to initialize.
	- Buildings: Add Attack and Defense capacity through their operation. Higher levels unlock more powerful Actions.

General Principles:
	- The Attack value of the infiltrating nation is compared to the Defense value of the defending nation, and the Defense value of the province targeted for infiltration.
	- Attack is always a national value.
	- Defense is both a national and provincial value.
		- Provincial Defense is the national +/- the provincial value.
	- Transparency is a national, and a provincial value.
		- Higher transparency means more information is known.
		- See Transparency section for how to calculate.

Transparency:
	- Calculated on the relative value of infiltrator National Attack value vs the target National Defense value.
	- Expressed as the percentage by which infiltrator National Attack dwarfs target National Defense. Minimum: zero (0%).
	- If a target is 10% transparent to the attacker that means the attacker can see the easiest 10% of the total hidden 10%.
	- Hidden information, priority, and quantity calculation:
		- What information is visible though the espionage system [category name: category components]?	
			- Building-Locations: Which buildings exist in which provinces.
			- Province-Level Info: province stability, province happiness, province security, province population, province wealth.
			- Positions of Formations: In which provinces are non-reserve formations located.
			- Target's COINTEL: Which provinces have Suppress Foreign Operation active.
			- Target's Foreign Espionage: which nations are being targeted within the espionage system by the target.
		- List of information by ease [easiest to hardest]: {Building-Locations, Province-Level Info, Positions of Formations, Target's COINTEL, Target's Foreign Espionage}
		- How is "quantity of information" calculated for "easiest x %"?
			- Take the total amount of available information the target has that could be obtained by 100% Transparency, then return the % that the target is actually Transparent. Building locations are easiest within the most populous provinces and hardest in the least populous. Other categories follow a similar trend. Target's Foreign Espionage is given only if there is enough transparency to cover an entire increment, and the resulting operation is chosen at random from the list. Actions:
	- Foreign:
		- Investigate Province: Massively boosts National Attack, but only when calculating transparency for the targeted province.
		- Promote Foreign Ideology: Causes unhappy or unsecure populations be more unstable. Also generates base instability.
		- Terrorist Attack: Kill a small number of pops and cause a massive temporary decrease to security.
		- Sabotage Building: Take a specific building in a specific province offline for a time. Length of disablement determined by Transparency, with greater Transparency yielding a longer time.
