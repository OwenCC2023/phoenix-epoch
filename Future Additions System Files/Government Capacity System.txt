Bureaucratic Capacity System

Main Goal: Require growth of bureaucratic capacity to implement more expansive population support programs and intensive regulatory activities.

General Principles:
	- Certain buildings generate Bureaucratic Capacity.
	- Policies above a certain tier consume Bureaucratic Capacity.
	- Governments are limited in their ability to implement support or regulatory systems based on the capabilities of their own internal structures.
	- Business-focused policies are less cumbersome than individual-focused policies.

Components:
	- Policy consumption gate: consumption occurs on top two tiers if more than 3 total tiers, otherwise top tier only.
	- If there is insufficient Bureaucratic Capacity to support the policy, then it cannot be enacted.
	

Tie-ins with Other Systems:
	- Buildings:
	- Policies:
		- Ecologists have no costs to pro-environment policies.
		- There are no costs for feminist, anti-slavery, or racial-equity policies.
		- All others employ this principle: consumption occurs progressively on higher tiers if more than 3 total tiers, otherwise top tier only. Top tier always gets max cost regardless of number of proceeding tiers.
	- Government Options:
		- Top-Down gets a big multiplicative bonus to Bureaucratic Capacity.
		- Collectivist gets a small multiplicative bonus to Bureaucratic Capacity.
		- Representative gets a small multiplicative bonus to Bureaucratic Capacity.
		- Singular gets a big multiplicative bonus to Bureaucratic Capacity.
		- Elections gets a small multiplicative bonus to Bureaucratic Capacity.
		
		- Resource gets a small multiplicative malus to Bureaucratic Capacity.
		- Military Power gets a small multiplicative malus to Bureaucratic Capacity.
		- Multi-Body gets a small multiplicative malus to Bureaucratic Capacity.
		- Staggered Groups gets a big multiplicative malus to Bureaucratic Capacity.
	- Ideologies:
		- Modern gets a multiplicative bonus to Bureaucratic Capacity.
		- Internationalist gets a multiplicative cost-reduction to treaty costs.
		- Libertarian gets a multiplicative malus to Bureaucratic Capacity.
		- Ecologists have no costs to pro-environment policies.
	- Diplomacy (stub):
		- Entering into and enforcing treaties takes bureaucratic capacity.
