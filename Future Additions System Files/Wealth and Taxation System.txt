Wealth and Taxation System

Main Goal: Create a quantitative measurement of the capabilities of a nation in aggregate, and the proportion of those capabilities available to the state.

General Principles:
	- Capacity of pops in province to do work.
	- Positively modified by buildings as they increase the value of each unit of work.
	- Modified positively/negatively by deficit/surplus of produced goods: supply_factor. When a good is in short supply the work done to produce it is more valuable proportional to the shortage amount, and vice versa.
	- Modified positively/negatively by surplus/deficit of required goods: input_factor. When the materials required to do work are in short supply the work is diminished in value proportional to the shortage, and vice versa.

Components:
	- Cost of a good is expressed in units of food needed to produce it. Input goods took people to make, and there are workers making the good, and both take food to maintain.
	- Value of a good is the 

	- Base prices in units of food: V(good) = FOOD_CONSUMPTION_PER_POP / (SUBSISTENCE_RATE × terrain_mult × productivity)
	- Formula for manufactured goods: V(good) = [Σ(input_qty_i × V(input_i)) + workers × FOOD_CONSUMPTION_PER_POP] ÷ (output_qty × productivity) × supply_factor × input_factor.
		- productivity = amount × effective_capacity × designation_mult × efficiency_mult × productivity_mult × dp_mult
	- Research, Literacy, Security, Happiness, and Stability are "manufactured" by buildings, but have no economic value. They still cost to produce, but no economic value is generated by their production.
	- Prices are computed dynamically on an annual basis (same cadence as DP-allocation).
