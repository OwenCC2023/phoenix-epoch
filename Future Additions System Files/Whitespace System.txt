Whitespace System

Main Goal: Allow provincial changes and ideologies of now-fallen nations to persist after their demise.

General Principles:
	- Some provinces exist outside of nations - these are colored white on the world map and called whitespace.
	- Provinces outside nations still have populations that are stable.
	- Ideologies outside nations are not consistent across provinces, but the provinces next to each other tend to have similar ideologies.
	- Provinces outside nations can become parts of nations.
	- Provinces that are a part of a nation can become outside of national control if the nation they are part of collapses, or a peace deal requires it, or the nation gives up their control over that province.
	- Provinces that leave nations retain the influences of the ideologies of that nation for a time.
	- Provinces next to, but are not a part of, nations get influenced ideologically by the nation's ideology.
	- Provinces all start the game with randomly assigned ideologies. Those that are part of the starting territory of a nation get the provincial ideologies overridden at game start by the national ideologies.

Components:
	- Conversion back to whitespace works like the Provincial Integration System's Normalization function, but in reverse.
	- Cross-Provincial Ideological Melding where nearby ideologies influence each other. Higher-pop provinces exert more ideological pressure on low-pop provinces.
	- Whitespace provinces have populations (assumed stable at the randomly-determined level appropriate for the province type).
	- Whitespace populations can migrate (stub for inclusion in International Migration System).
	- Whitespace provinces are assumed to have little in the way of centralized government, economies, militaries, or infrastructure.

Tie-ins with Other Systems:
	- Buildings:
		- None
	- Policies:
		- Migration/Visa policies (stub for International Migration System).
	- Ideologies:
		- Each province has a random set of ideologies that adhere to ideology-selection rules that are assigned at game start and vary over time.
		- Provinces with militarist and nationalist ideologies are more likely to spawn rebels when expanded into (stub for Control and Rebellion System).
